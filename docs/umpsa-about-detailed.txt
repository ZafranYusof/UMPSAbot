UMPSA About - University History and Background

Established as a technical university in 2002, Universiti Malaysia Pahang Al-Sultan Abdullah (UMPSA) offers various engineering- and technology-based technical programmes, including high-level Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET) programmes.

History:
- 16 February 2002: Established as Kolej Universiti Kejuruteraan & Teknologi Malaysia (KUKTEM)
- 1 February 2007: Renamed to Universiti Malaysia Pahang (UMP)
- 14 April 2023: Branded as Universiti Malaysia Pahang Al-Sultan Abdullah (UMPSA) based on the Universiti Malaysia Pahang Order (Incorporation) (Amendment) 2023 (P.U. (A) 116/2023)

After 23 years of glory, the university is now branded as UMPSA. His Royal Highness, The Sultan of Pahang, Al-Sultan Abdullah Ri'ayatuddin Al-Mustafa Billah Shah, who is also the UMPSA Chancellor, contributed a lot in shaping the country leadership, empowering education, disseminating knowledge and religion.

Key Achievements:
- Ranked as one of the best in research and innovation within MTUN and Non-Research University classifications
- Only public university (UA) that has implemented ten dual degree programmes
- Strategic international collaborations with universities in Germany, China, Kazakhstan, and South Korea

Dual Degree Partners:
- Karlsruhe University of Applied Sciences (HKA), Germany
- Mannheim University of Applied Sciences (HMA), Germany
- BJTU, China
- Tianjin University, China
- Kazakh National Agrarian Research University (KazNARU), Kazakhstan
- Hanyang University Erica Campus, South Korea

Research Focus:
UMPSA collaborates with local industries to focus on industry-related applications. Such research collaboration enriches the teaching and learning modules at the university and simultaneously promotes the commercialisation of research output and products.
