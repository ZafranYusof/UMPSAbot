UMPSA History and Background
=============================

ESTABLISHMENT
-------------
Universiti Malaysia Pahang Al-Sultan Abdullah (UMPSA) was established as a technical university in 2002. It offers various engineering- and technology-based technical programmes, including high-level Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET) programmes.

TIMELINE
--------
- 16 February 2002: Established as Kolej Universiti Kejuruteraan & Teknologi Malaysia (KUKTEM)
- 1 February 2007: Renamed to Universiti Malaysia Pahang (UMP) by the Government of Malaysia
- 14 April 2023: Branded as Universiti Malaysia Pahang Al-Sultan Abdullah (UMPSA) based on the Universiti Malaysia Pahang Order (Incorporation) (Amendment) 2023 (P.U. (A) 116/2023)

NAMING
------
The university is named after His Royal Highness, The Sultan of Pahang, Al-Sultan Abdullah Ri'ayatuddin Al-Mustafa Billah Shah Ibni Almarhum Sultan Haji Ahmad Shah Al-Musta'in Billah, who is also the UMPSA Chancellor. His Royal Highness contributed significantly in shaping country leadership, empowering education, disseminating knowledge and religion, and showing concern towards the people of Pahang Darul Makmur.

CLASSIFICATION
--------------
- Malaysia Technical University Network (MTUN) member
- Non-Research University (Non-RU) classification
- Ranked as one of the best in research and innovation within MTUN
- Focus: Engineering, technology, and industrial sciences

DUAL DEGREE PROGRAMMES
-----------------------
UMPSA is the only public university (UA) that has implemented ten dual degree programmes with strategic international partners:
- Karlsruhe University of Applied Sciences (HKA), Germany
- Mannheim University of Applied Sciences (HMA), Germany
- BJTU, China
- Tianjin University, China
- Kazakh National Agrarian Research University (KazNARU), Kazakhstan
- Hanyang University Erica Campus, South Korea

RESEARCH FOCUS
--------------
- Collaborates with local industries on industry-related applications
- Research collaboration enriches teaching and learning modules
- Promotes commercialisation of research output and products
- Strategic international collaborations for academic programme innovation
