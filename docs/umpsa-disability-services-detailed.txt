UMPSA Student With Disability Services

UMP Disability Services Unit (OkUMP)

UMP has designated the Disability Services Unit (OkUMP) to determine eligibility for reasonable accommodations (academic adjustments, auxiliary aids, and modification) for students with disabilities. This unit acts as a governance coordinator to create an inclusive and conducive campus environment/ecosystem for disabled people, coordinating efficient and effective management of disabled people at all levels of the university administration.

Screen Reader Information:
- A screen reader converts text on the screen into speech so that visually impaired people can listen to what is happening on the screen.
- Download any screen reader and install. Different screen readers have different styles/features.
- Once installed, you can turn it on or off, or set it to automatically turn on every time you power on your computer.

Flow of Use:
- Screen reader converts text to speech
- When users browse a website, the screen reader will automatically read the whole website from top left to bottom right
- Users can stop anywhere by pressing a specific key shortcut (for NVDA, press "Ctrl" to stop)
- Users can press the next button to read web elements one by one
- Users can skip to the next landmark, title, heading, link, button, or image
- For input forms, press "Enter" to change into focus mode to type
- To use a button or enter a link, press "Enter"
- Some applications have their own shortcut keys (e.g., Shift+V to turn on camera in Zoom)

Disability Friendly University:
- Disability is not an obstacle to becoming a member of the UMP community
- Most buildings have access for disabled people
- University is constantly being built, reconstructed, adjusted, and adapted to the needs of students with physical disabilities
- Students with disabilities live in dormitory rooms adapted to their needs, with a possibility for companions to be accommodated together
- Many academic programmes are flexible and can be adapted to needs of students with disabilities (distance learning, various forms of examination)
- The university provides students with disabilities with learning or educational tools and devices
