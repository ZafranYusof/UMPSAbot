UMPSA Examination Information Guide
=====================================

EXAMINATION SYSTEM
------------------
UMPSA uses a continuous assessment and final examination system. Students are evaluated through coursework and final examinations each semester.

ASSESSMENT METHODS
------------------
1. Continuous Assessment (Coursework):
   - Assignments
   - Quizzes
   - Lab reports
   - Projects
   - Presentations
   - Mid-semester tests
   - Tutorials

2. Final Examination:
   - Held at the end of each semester
   - Duration varies by course (typically 2-3 hours)
   - Scheduled by the university examination unit

EXAMINATION SCHEDULE
--------------------
- Final examination schedule is published on E-Community (E-Comm) portal
- Students can check their personal exam timetable via E-Comm
- Examination period is specified in the Academic Calendar
- Typically held in the last 2-3 weeks of each semester

EXAMINATION RULES AND REGULATIONS
-----------------------------------
1. Students MUST bring their student ID card (matric card) to all examinations
2. Students must be seated at least 15 minutes before the exam starts
3. No entry allowed after 30 minutes from the start of the examination
4. No electronic devices (phones, smartwatches) allowed in the exam hall
5. Only approved materials (calculator, drawing tools) as specified by the course lecturer
6. Students must not communicate with other students during the examination
7. Any form of cheating will result in disciplinary action

ACADEMIC STANDING
-----------------
- Good Standing: CGPA 2.00 and above
- Conditional (Bersyarat): CGPA below 2.00
- Academic Probation: Two consecutive semesters with CGPA below 2.00
- Dismissal: May occur after continued poor academic performance

GPA AND CGPA CALCULATION
--------------------------
- GPA (Grade Point Average): Calculated per semester
- CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average): Calculated across all semesters
- Formula: Sum of (Credit Hours x Grade Points) / Total Credit Hours

SPECIAL EXAMINATION
-------------------
- Students who miss examinations due to valid reasons (medical, emergency) may apply for special examination
- Must provide supporting documents (medical certificate, etc.)
- Application must be submitted within specified timeframe
- Approved by the Faculty/Department

EXAMINATION RESULTS
-------------------
- Results are released on E-Community (E-Comm) portal
- Students can view results after the official release date
- Any queries about results should be directed to the respective faculty

ACADEMIC INTEGRITY
------------------
- Plagiarism and cheating are serious offences
- Penalties range from zero marks to expulsion
- All work submitted must be original
- Proper citation required for referenced materials

IMPORTANT PORTALS
-----------------
- E-Community (E-Comm): For exam schedule, results, and academic records
- Access via: https://ecomm.ump.edu.my
