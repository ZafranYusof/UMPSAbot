ASEAN International Mobility for Students (AIMS) Programme at UMPSA

The ASEAN International Mobility for Students (AIMS) Programme is a regional student mobility initiative coordinated by SEAMEO RIHED, aimed at strengthening academic cooperation and people-to-people connectivity among higher education institutions in ASEAN and selected partner countries.

The programme originated in 2009 as the Malaysia-Indonesia-Thailand (M-I-T) Student Mobility Pilot Project and has since expanded significantly. As of 2025, AIMS involves participation from multiple ASEAN member states and partner countries, including Malaysia, Indonesia, Thailand, Vietnam, the Philippines, Brunei Darussalam, Japan, and the Republic of Korea. The programme supports short-term, credit-bearing student mobility across a wide range of academic disciplines.

Host University Support:
Under the AIMS Programme, the Host University generally provides:
- Tuition fee waiver
- Access to basic medical services (campus or in-house health centres)
- Assistance in arranging student accommodation
- Student support services such as buddy, tutoring, and counselling programmes

Financial Support:
Financial support under the AIMS Programme is typically provided by the Home Government / Home University, subject to national policies and availability of funds. This support may include:
- Monthly living allowance
- International airfare (economy class, subject to approval)
- Visa and insurance support
- Other allowable mobility-related expenses, depending on the funding scheme

The type and amount of financial assistance may vary by country and institution.
